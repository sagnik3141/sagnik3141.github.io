Research

My research interests include computational imaging, computer vision and machine learning. My goal is to design smarter imaging systems by developining algorithms that work in unison with novel sensors. My undergraduate research is focussed on developing stereo matching algorithms for Single-Photon Cameras and Lensless Cameras.

Lensless Stereo Imaging
[project page] [code]

This project is about predicting a disparity estimate from a pair of lensless measurements. We train a trainable inversion layer to undo the multiplexing followed by a stereo network end-to-end. We perform comparisons with wiener deconvolution followed by a stereo network on full and cropped lensless measurements and demostrate that our network is compaetitive with a two-stage method (image reconstruction followed by disparity estimation).

Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models
[session recording]

I conducted a session on a class of generative models called Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPMs) at the Image Processing and Computer Vision Lab, IIT Madras. This session introduces the mathematical formulation behind DDPMs, conditional generation with DDPMs, and some recent advances like text-to-image with DDPMs (DALLE-2), superresolution and adversarial purification using DDPMs.
